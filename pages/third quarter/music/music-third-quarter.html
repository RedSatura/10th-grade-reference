3.1: Contemporary Music in the Philippines

Contemporary Music in the Philippines
Contemporary Music in the Philippines usually refers to compositions that have adopted ideas and elements from 20th century art music in the West.
The modern Filipino repetoire consists of pieces that have evolved out of stylistic movements such as expressionism, neo-classism, and avant-garde.
The first modern works are attributed to Nicanor Abelardo, who produced works such as "Sinfonietta for Strings" and the "Cinderella Overture".
His chamber music suite, "Panoramas", was an experiment on unorthodox instrumental combinations.
Other similar works include "Dancing Fool" and "Malikmata" by Antonio Molina, "Philippine Suite" by Ramon Tapales, and "Mindanao Sketches" by Antonio Buenaventura.
Philippine neo-classism is represented by three names" Eliseo Pajaro, Rosendo Santos and Lucresia Kasilag.
Pajaro's works are set in extended formats such as the symphony, concerto, and symphonic ode.
Some examples of his works are "Academic Freedom", and "Himig Iloko", in which song cycles with Filipino folk tunes are used.
Rosendo Santos, a versatile performer, able to play keyboard, percussion, and wind instruments, has written hundreds of compositions for a variety of instruments.
Lucresia Kasilag has added a further dimension to her compositios by infusing the sounds of native instruments.
Some of her works include "Her Son, Jose", "Dularawan", "Toccata for Percussion (Orthodox and Muslim) and Winds", and "Orientalis Suite for Piano and Chamber and Philippines Percussion Instruments".

3.2: Composers of Contemporary Philippine Music

  • Antonio Molina
    National Artist for Music in 1973, known as the "Claude Debussy of the Philippines".
  • Lucrecia Kasilag
    National Artist for Music in 1989, First Lady of Philippine Music.
  • Lucio San Pedro
    National Artist for Music in 1991, known for his creative nationalism.
  • Jose Maceda
    National Artist for Music in 1997, ethnomusicologist who specializes in Indonesian and Philippine music.
  • Ernani Cuenco
    National Artist for Music in 1999.
  • Francisco Feliciano
    National Artist for Music in 2014.
  • Ryan Cayabyab
    National Artist for Music in 2018, known as "Mr. C".
  • Levi Celerio
    National Artist for Literature and Music in 1997
  • Constancio de Guzman
    Dean of Filipino movie composers and musical directors.
  • Restituto "Restie" Umali
    A popular film score composer and musical director whose main works were scenes for motion pictures.
  • George Conseco
    Has won a total of 6 FAMAS Awards for Best Movie Theme Song.
  • Chino Toledo
    His own music is characterized by great energy and often bears a relationship to the traditional music of Southeast Asia.
  • Rosendo Santos Jr.
    Received the title of "Philippine Composerof the Century".
  • Antonio Buenaventura
    Conducted research on Philippine folk songs and dances, brining folk music closer to the young people of today's generation.
  • Ramon Santos
    He was so amazed with the avant-garde music of Varese.
