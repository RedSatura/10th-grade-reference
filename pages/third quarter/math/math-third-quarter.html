3.1: The Circle and its Parts

The Circle
A circle is a set of all points in a plane that are equidistant from a fixed point.
The fixed point is called the center of the circle, and the constant distance is the radius.
In addition, a circle is named after its center.

Parts of a Circle

Parts of a Circle
  • A radius of a circle is a segment whose endpoints are the center and a point of the circle. It is sometimes referred to as the distance from the center of circle to a point on the circle.
  • A chord of a circle is a line segment that has its endpoints on the circle.
  • A diameter of a circle is a chord that passes through the center of the circle. Its length is twice as long as a radius.
  • A tangent is a line or a ray that intersects a circle at exactly one point, and intersection is called the point of tangency.
  • A secant is a line, a segment or a ray that intersects a circle at exactly two points.
Things to Remember
  • All radii are congruent.
  • Every chord being a line segment determines a secant.
  • Every secant, being a line, contains a chord.

3.2: Central Angles

Central Angles
The central angle is an angle whose vertex is at center of the circle and whose sides are radii of the circle.
An Arc is a part of a circle which includes any two points. It can be classified asminor arc, major arc, and semicircle.
An arc is denoted by the symbol “⌒” place above the endpoints that form the arc.
The measurement of the intercepted arc or an arc is equal to the measurement of the corresponding central angle.
Classification of Arcs Measurements Naming Convention
Minor Arc Measures more than 0° but less than 180°. A minor arc is named by using only two points, which are the two endpoints of the arc.
Semicircle Measures exactly 180°. A semicircle is named using three points.
The first and the third points are the endpoints of the diameter.
The second point is any point on the circle between these endpoints.
Major Arc Measures more than 180° but less than 360°. A major arc is named by using three points in the same way that we name a semicircle.

3.3: Inscribed Angles

Inscribed Angles
An inscribed angle is an angle formed by the intersection of two chords and a point on the circle.
Inscribed Angle Theorem
The measure of an inscribed angle is equal to one- half the measure of its intercepted arc.
Things to Remember
  • An angle inscribed in a semicircle is a right triangle.
  • Angles inscribed in the same arc are congruent.
  • The opposite angles of an inscribed quadrilateral are supplementary.

3.4: The Tangent-Secant Theorem

The Tangent-Secant Theorem
Given an angle with its vertex on a circle, formed by a secant ray and a tangent ray, the measure of the angle is half the measure of the intercepted arc.
Intersecting Chords Theorem
If two chords intersect within a circle, then the measure of the angle formed is equal to half the sum of the measures of the intercepted arc.

3.5: Angles Formed in the Exterior of a Circle

Angles Formed in the Exterior of a Circle
If a tangent and a secant, two secants or two tangents intersect at a point in the exterior of a circle, the measure of the angle formed is equal to half the difference of the measures of the intercepted arcs.
