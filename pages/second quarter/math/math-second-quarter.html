2.2: Dividing Polynomials

Dividing Polynomials
When dividing two polynomials, the result is a quotient and remainder.
If the remainder is 0, then the divisor is a factor of a dividend.

2.2.1: Long Division Method

Long Division Method
  • Step 1: Arrange the dividend and divisor in descending order of the degree of the terms involved and insert zeroes as coefficients of any missing term.
  • Step 2: Divide the first term of the dividend by the first term of the divisor and write the quotient.
  • Step 3: Multiply the quotient in Step 2 by the whole divisor and write it in the second row of the dividend. Align the terms based on their degree.
  • Step 4: Subtract the dividend and the second row.
  • Step 5: Repeat steps 2 to 4 until the final remainder is zero or a lower degree polynomial than the divisor.

2.2.2: Synthetic Division Method

Synthetic Division Method
Synthetic division is the shortcut process of dividing with coefficients only in a special format.
  • Step 1: Arrange the dividend and divisor in descending order of the degree of the terms involved and insert zeroes as coefficients of any missing term.
  • Step 2: Get the root of the divisor, and place the result in the division box.
  • Step 3: Bring down the leading coefficient to the bottom row.
  • Step 4: Multiply the number in the bottom row by the number in the division box, and place the result in the next column right below the coefficient.
  • Step 5: Repeat steps 3 and 4 until you get through all coefficients.
