2.1: Computer Generated Imagery

Computer Generated Imagery
Computer-generated imagery (CGI) is the use of computer graphics to create or contribute to images in art.

2.1.1: Equipment used in CGI

Equipment used in CGI
  • Overhead Projector
    An overhead projector is a machine that has a light inside it and makes the writing orpictures on a sheet of plastic appear on a screen or wall.
  • Duplicating Machine
    A duplicating machine is a device for making duplicate copies from a master copy of printed, typed, drawn, or other material and utilizing various reproduction techniques to this end.
  • Laser Scanner
    This is used for land surveying methods that can accurately measure and collect data from objects, surfaces, buildings, and landscapes.
  • Thermal and Electrostatic Photocopier
    It is a machine that makes copies of documents and other visual images onto paper or plastic film quickly and cheaply.
  • Digital Camera
    It is a camera similar to traditional film-based cameras, but it captures images digitally.
    Unlike film cameras, digital cameras can easily display images on a screen immediately after bing recorded, and store and delete images from memory.
  • Cellular Phone
    These are mobile phones that include advanced functionality beyond making phone calls and sending text messages.
    Most smartphones have the capacity to displaty photos, play videos, and surf the Web.

2.2: Digital Art

Digital Art
Digital art is an artistic work or practice that uses digital technology as an essential part of the creative or presentation process.
Digital art can be purely computer-generated such as a scanned photograph or an image drawn using vector graphics software using a mouse or graphics tablet.

2.2.1: Kinds of Digital Art

Kinds of Digital Art
  • Graphic illustrations
    These are used in advertising, logos, brochures, and posters.
  • Illustrations
    These are used in books, comic, and web sites.
  • 3D Models
    These are used in animations, movies, and video games.
  • Digital Photos
    It is an artwork used to create posters and advertisements.
  • Digital Painting
    It is an emerging art form in which traditional painting techniques such as watercolor, oils, and impasto are applied using digital tools by means of a computer, a graphics tablet and stylus, and software.
  • Websites
    These refer to the collections of web pages and related contents that are identified by a common domain name and published on at least one web server.
  • Computer-generated Images
    These require application of computer graphics to create or to contribute to images in art, printed media, video games, film, television programs, and so on.
  • Animation
    It is a method in which figures are manipulated to appear as moving images.
  • Games
    These involve interaction with the user interface or input device, such as a joystick, controller, keyboard, or motion sensing devices, to generate visual, feedback on two or three-dimensional video display device.
